Task given

Build a leakage-safe, time-aware training pipeline and report an honest offline metric for a claims model.

What the candidate submitted
Time-based split (no shuffle); fit scalers/encoders inside the CV fold on train only; GroupKFold by member id to prevent identity leakage; reported PR-AUC with a calibration curve; added a drift check on feature distributions across the split boundary.
Evaluator notes (human-reviewed)

Time-based split and in-fold transforms done correctly — the classic leakage trap avoided. Grouping by member id was the right call for this domain. Calibration and the drift check were strong additions. Did not parallelize CV; fine at this scale, flagged.

Interviewer (AI)
Your model influences a benefit decision for a member. How do you make that defensible to a compliance reviewer?
Rohan
Every decision has to be explainable and reproducible. I log the model version, the features and their values, and the score, so a reviewer can reconstruct exactly why a decision was made. I keep PHI out of the inputs unless it's required and approved, and I run bias checks across protected segments before release with the results in a model card. The model informs; a human decides — the way a credit model supports an officer rather than replacing the judgment.
Interviewer (AI)
Say a bias check flags a disparity after launch. What's your move?
Rohan
Don't silently retrain. Quantify the disparity, decide whether it's the model or the data, and bring it to the owners with options — threshold adjustment, reweighting, or pulling the feature — and the trade-offs stated. Shipping a change is documented, not unilateral.
Reviewer rationale
  • Anchored on reproducibility and audit trail — the right bar for a regulated AI decision.
  • Kept PHI handling conservative and approval-gated.
  • Framed the model as decision support with a human in the loop, not an autonomous decision-maker.
