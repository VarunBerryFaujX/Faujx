Interviewer (AI)
Walk me through a time you cut a system's latency. What was the bottleneck and how did you find it?
Maya
Checkout p95 was ~2 seconds. I traced it with distributed spans and found a synchronous call to a fraud service inside the request path. I moved it to an async event and let the order proceed optimistically, reconciling on the callback. p95 dropped to about 600ms and the peak-sale error rate fell roughly 70%.
Interviewer (AI)
What did you give up by going optimistic, and how did you cover it?
Maya
I accepted a small window where a flagged order could slip through, so I added a compensating reversal on the callback and a daily reconciliation job. For our fraud rate the exposure was negligible against the conversion gain, and finance signed off on the reconciliation control.
Reviewer rationale
  • Diagnoses from evidence (spans) rather than guessing — strong systems instinct.
  • Explicitly named the trade-off and the compensating control, not just the win.
  • Communicated a technical decision in business terms (conversion vs. exposure).
