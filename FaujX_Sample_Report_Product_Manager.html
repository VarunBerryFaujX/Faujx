Interviewer (AI)
Three stakeholders each want their feature first and you can only ship one. How do you handle the room?
Carlos
I don't arbitrate on volume. I bring it back to the outcome we agreed to move this quarter — activation — and score each ask on expected impact against effort. Then I make the call transparently and write down why, including what we're deliberately not doing and when we'll revisit it. People accept a 'no' far better when the reasoning and the revisit date are explicit.
Interviewer (AI)
What if the data to justify the call doesn't exist yet?
Carlos
Then I don't pretend it does. I design the smallest experiment that would change my mind, time-box it, and define the success metric up front. A two-week test beats a quarter of debate, and it turns a stakeholder fight into a question we can answer.
Reviewer rationale
  • Anchors conflict to a single agreed outcome rather than politics — senior judgment.
  • Comfortable saying no with documented reasoning and a revisit date.
  • Defaults to small experiments over opinion; honest about absent data.
