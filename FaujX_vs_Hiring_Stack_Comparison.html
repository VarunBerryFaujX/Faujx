Platform by platform

Where each one ends, and FaujX begins

Honest framing, because the strongest position is the true one: each of these is good at its layer. FaujX is the layer above.

FaujX vs. Workday

The system of record vs. the decision layer

Workday is the HCM and ATS system of record — requisitions, workflow, AI matching and ranking, conversational screening.

Workday organizes and moves the process. It surfaces and ranks candidates; it was not built to validate whether a candidate can do the specific role. FaujX produces the capability verdict that Workday's workflow then carries.

Relationship · integrates via API
FaujX vs. Greenhouse

Structured process vs. structured proof

Greenhouse is the structured-hiring ATS — scorecards, interview kits, consistent process, deep integrations.

Greenhouse structures how your people evaluate; by design its AI doesn't score or decide. FaujX produces the validated capability evidence before the loop, so the scorecards your team fills in start from proof, not a résumé.

Relationship · integrates via API
FaujX vs. Phenom

Experience & engagement vs. capability & verdict

Phenom is the talent-experience layer — career sites, CRM, talent marketing, internal mobility, fit-score matching.

Phenom gets the right candidates to apply and keeps them engaged. It scores fit and personalizes; it doesn't run a governed evaluation to a hire / no-hire call. FaujX takes the engaged pool and tells you who clears the bar.

FaujX vs. HackerRank

Deep coding signal vs. whole-role signal

HackerRank is a leading technical-assessment platform — large question library, real-time IDE, adaptive AI interviewer, integrity signals.

For engineering coding depth, HackerRank is specialist and strong. But it answers one slice of one role family. FaujX validates the whole role — across screening, interview, and expert review — for every role, technical or not.

FaujX vs. Codility

Coding tests vs. the consolidated decision

Codility is a technical-assessment platform — real-world coding tasks, plagiarism detection, async and live modes, broad language support.

Codility produces a rigorous coding score for engineers. FaujX folds a role-calibrated challenge into a full pipeline and a single capability verdict — so the coding result is one input to the decision, not the whole of it.

The pattern

Tracking, experience, and testing all stop short of the same answer

Each tool collects something true about a candidate. None of them resolves it into can this person do this job. That gap — between everything your stack knows and the decision your hiring manager has to make — is the only thing FaujX is built to close.

Your stack already tells you who applied. FaujX tells you who can do the job.

FHOS runs a single structured pipeline on every candidate — ingestion, capability intelligence, adaptive screening, an optional role challenge, a structured AI interview, and expert human review — and returns one decision-ready report.

It connects to Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever via API, runs white-labeled under your brand, and leaves every decision with your hiring manager. Roughly 80% of the hire / no-hire signal is produced before a single live interview.
