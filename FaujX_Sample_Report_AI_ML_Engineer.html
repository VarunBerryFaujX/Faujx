Interviewer (AI)
A model that performed well at launch is quietly degrading in production. How do you investigate?
Dev
First I separate data drift from concept drift. I compare the live feature distributions and the label distribution against the training window — usually it's an upstream feature that changed meaning or a population shift. If features are stable but accuracy fell, it's concept drift and I look at retraining cadence.
Interviewer (AI)
Say it's a feature that silently changed. What do you do before retraining?
Dev
I'd freeze and version the change, backfill a corrected feature if possible, and quantify impact on the metric before touching the model — retraining on contaminated data just bakes the error in. Then retrain with the corrected feature and validate against a holdout from after the change.
Reviewer rationale
  • Separated data vs. concept drift unprompted — a senior diagnostic frame.
  • Resisted the reflex to retrain immediately; fixed the data first.
  • Tied each step to the metric, showing evaluation discipline over tooling talk.
